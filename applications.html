Applications of Generative AI in the Real World

The use of generative AI in hiring and recruitment has become increasingly common as organizations search for faster and efficient ways to manage extensive numbers of applying applicants. One substantial application is resume screening and candidate matching. Generative AI systems have the capabilities that include analyzing resumes, summarizing applicant qualifications, and matching candidates to specific job descriptions based on the skills and experience their resumes provide. Many companies now depend on automated tools to screen candidates because of the high number of applications, with studies showing that a significant portion of major organizations use some form of AI in their hiring processes. This application proposes important social challenges. Hiring decisions directly impact an individual’s career and future opportunities. The challenge with this technology is that it learns from past data. If that data includes bias, the AI can end up repeating those same patterns. For example, a few studies have revealed that AI hiring tools have the possibility to reflect racial or gender bias when it comes to ranking candidates. This poses a serious issue for it makes it harder to ensure that the process is truly fair even if it seems objective. Another crucial application is the use of AI-powered interview tools and chatbots. Companies use generative AI to create interview questions, run initial screenings, and communicate with applicants. These tools can be efficient in speeding things up and making it easier to handle substantial numbers of applying candidates. In many cases, AI also can help assist recruiters in reviewing applications or even sorting them before a person takes a closer look. Even with these benefits, there are still many concerns. Applicants often are not aware of when AI is being used or how their answers are being observed. This can make the process feel unclear and less trustworthy. On top of that, AI can misunderstand responses or even overlook individual differences, which can lead to unfair results. Government agencies like the Equal Opportunity Commission (EEOC) have also warned that these tools can lead to bigger discriminatory practices even if it's not intentional. Overall, the use of generative AI in hiring matters because employment decisions directly impact people’s lives. The challenge is finding the right balance between efficiency and fairness, while making sure these systems don't reinforce existing inequalities.
