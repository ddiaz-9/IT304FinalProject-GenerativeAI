Generative Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Within the fields of information technology and businesses, several tools have risen in popularity based on their utilization and benefits brought to users. Notably, the technology of generative artificial intelligence (AI) is a prominent tool that has been widely implemented throughout the work environment, displaying a rise in significance. Generative AI is a tool in which content is generated from user input and its output ranges from video, audio, and text based responses (IBM, n.d). Its process relies on three components for operation, those being training, tuning, and generation/evaluation/more tuning for enhanced use. As businesses vary in goals and objectives, it is important for individuals to adjust the technology based on its use case as well, for example, in customer service, generative AI usage should focus on assisting customer requests in an efficient and accurate manner, therefore providing automation for simple tasks when needed. It has also been noted how the adoption of generative AI will exceed 80% in 2026 from various applications (IBM, n.d).

Reasoning and Importance

The reasoning for the selection of generative AI pertains to its many benefits and increasing utilization in the business environment. Part of that includes how AI has rapidly advanced over the past few years, with many people are resorting to the technology for assistance with tasks. Plus, its primary advantage is efficiency due to content generation providing immediate results for users, allowing for the capabilities of automation, lowering expenses, and increasing productivity (IBM, n.d). Its versatility becomes more apparent with different applications such as customer service, software development, and digital labor, all of which incorporate automation and efficiency as a major component.
